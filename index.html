Discover Guernsey's Best-Kept Secret

A secluded south coast cove with crystal-clear water, perfect for snorkeling and escaping the crowds.

About Le Jaonnet Bay

Le Jaonnet Bay remains one of Guernsey's hidden treasures. Unlike the bustling west coast beaches, this small cove rewards those willing to seek it out with tranquility and some of the clearest water on the island.

The bay is tucked between rocky outcrops, creating a natural sheltered swimming area. The water here achieves remarkable clarity - on calm days, you can see the sandy bottom and darting fish from the surface. It's no wonder snorkelers consider this their favorite spot.

The Path Less Traveled

Reaching Le Jaonnet requires descending a steep, narrow path - this natural filter keeps the beach peaceful even in peak summer. The journey down takes about 10 minutes and requires sensible footwear.

Marine Life

Bring snorkel gear. The rocky edges of the bay host spider crabs, blennies, and occasional wrasse. At low tide, the exposed rock pools reveal sea anemones, starfish, and periwinkles.

Visitor Tips

  • Arrive early - only 15-20 parking spaces nearby
  • Pack a picnic - no cafes or shops within walking distance
  • Wear reef shoes - rocks can be slippery
  • Check tide times - beach disappears at high tide
  • Take rubbish home - no bins available
